INTRODUCTIONS & NETWORKING

Business English — B1 Level

Scenario

🏢 Networking Event

You're at a business networking event in Vienna. People from various companies are present. You need to introduce yourself, make small talk, and exchange business cards.

Your goal: Introduce yourself professionally, ask questions to learn about others, and make a memorable connection.

Key Phrases

"Nice to meet you. I'm [Name], and I work as a [role] at [company]."

Use: When meeting someone for the first time in a professional setting.

"What brings you to this event?"

Use: To show interest in the other person and start a conversation.

"That sounds interesting. Could you tell me more about what your company does?"

Use: To keep the conversation going and show genuine curiosity.

"I'd love to stay in touch. Here's my business card."

Use: To end the conversation professionally and exchange contact details.

Example Dialogue

Anna (Marketing Manager)

Hi, I'm Anna. I work in marketing at TechVision. What about you?

Thomas (Software Developer)

Nice to meet you, Anna. I'm Thomas. I'm a developer at CodeCraft. What brings you to this event today?

Anna

I'm looking to connect with people in the tech industry. We're launching a new product next month, and I'm interested in potential partnerships. How about you?

Thomas

That sounds fascinating. We're always open to collaboration. Could you tell me more about the product?

Anna

Of course! It's a project management tool designed for remote teams. I'd love to get your perspective as a developer. Maybe we could schedule a coffee meeting next week?

Thomas

That would be great. Here's my card. Let me know what works for you.

Language Focus

Formal vs Informal Register

Formal (business): "It's a pleasure to meet you. I'd like to introduce myself."

Informal (casual): "Hey, nice to meet you! I'm Anna."

💡 At networking events, start with formal and become more relaxed as the conversation develops.

Follow-up Questions

• "What do you enjoy most about your role?"

• "How long have you been working in this field?"

• "What are the biggest challenges your company faces right now?"
