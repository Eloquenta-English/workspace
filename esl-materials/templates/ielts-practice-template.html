3. What kind of tourist destinations do you usually prefer?

💭 Think for 10 seconds, then answer in 3 sentences with reasons.

✅ Model Answer (3 sentences with reasons)
I generally prefer historical cities over beach destinations because I enjoy learning about local culture and architecture. Places like Rome or Prague are ideal for me because you can walk around and discover something new around every corner. I also like destinations where you can try traditional food, since that's an important part of experiencing a culture.

4. What's the best way to save money while travelling?

💭 Think for 10 seconds, then answer in 3 sentences with reasons.

✅ Model Answer (3 sentences with reasons)
I think the most effective way to save money is to book flights and accommodation well in advance, because prices increase significantly during peak season. Another useful strategy is to use public transport instead of taxis, which is not only cheaper but also gives you a more authentic experience of the city. Finally, eating at local restaurants rather than tourist spots can save you a lot of money while often giving you better food.
Part 2 — Long Turn

Cue Card

Describe a journey you have been on.
  • Where you went
  • What kind of transport you used
  • How long the journey took
  • Say whether you think it was easy to take this journey or not, and why
⏱️ 1 min prepare ⏱️ 2 min speak
✅ Model Answer (2 minutes)
I'd like to talk about a train journey I took from Vienna to Salzburg last summer. I chose to travel by train because it's more comfortable than driving, and you can enjoy the scenery through the window without having to concentrate on the road. The journey took about two and a half hours, which I think is quite reasonable for that distance. I found it very easy and relaxing because the train was clean, punctual, and I had time to read a book and listen to music. The only slightly challenging part was that I had to change trains in Linz, but overall it was a very pleasant experience, and I'd definitely choose the train again for the same route.
Part 3 — Discussion

Abstract Questions

1. How do most people travel long distances in your country?

💭 Think about different groups of people. Give a balanced answer.

✅ Model Answer
In Austria, most people travel long distances by car because the road network is well-maintained and parking is generally available. However, there's been a significant shift towards train travel in recent years, especially since the introduction of the Klimaticket, which makes regional trains very affordable. Young people in particular are choosing trains for environmental reasons, while business travellers often prefer the convenience of driving.

2. Have the types of transport people use changed much over the last few decades?

💭 Compare past and present. Give specific examples.

✅ Model Answer
Yes, there have been significant changes. Thirty years ago, most people relied on private cars or buses for long distances, and air travel was much more expensive. Today, high-speed rail has become much more common, and budget airlines have made flying accessible to many more people. Additionally, we've seen the rise of ride-sharing services like Uber, which didn't exist at all before, and electric scooters have become popular in cities for short trips.

Abstract Questions

3. What kinds of obstacles do you think some local governments have to face when they develop transportation infrastructure?

💭 Think about money, politics, geography, and social impact.

✅ Model Answer
Local governments face several major obstacles. The most significant is funding, because infrastructure projects are extremely expensive and often exceed initial budgets. Political challenges also play a role, as new administrations may cancel or redirect projects started by predecessors. Geographical factors like mountains or rivers add technical complexity, and there's often resistance from local communities who don't want construction disruption in their neighborhoods.

4. Why do some people prefer to travel abroad rather than travel in their own country?

💭 Consider novelty, cost, climate, and cultural factors.

✅ Model Answer
People often prefer travelling abroad because of the novelty — experiencing different cultures, cuisines, and architectures feels more exciting than visiting familiar places. Climate is another factor; people from colder countries often travel somewhere warm during winter. Additionally, some people find that international travel offers better value for money, especially when domestic tourism is expensive. Finally, there's a certain social prestige associated with having visited other countries.
