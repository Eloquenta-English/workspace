Future Continuous & Perfect

will be + verb-ing / will have + pp
Actions in progress or completed at a future time

Examples - Future continuous

Future continuous

This time tomorrow, I will be hiking.

Examples - Future perfect

Future perfect

By next year, she will have graduated.

Examples - Combined

Combined

When you arrive, we will have finished dinner.

Common Mistakes

❌ "I will be finish by then."

✅ "I will have finished by then."

💡 Future perfect: will have + pp, not will be + base.

❌ "She will be graduate by June."

✅ "She will have graduated by June."

💡 Use will have + pp for completion.

❌ "They will be arrive by 5pm."

✅ "They will have arrived by 5pm."

💡 Will have + past participle.

Exercise

By next summer, I _____ (complete) my degree.

  • will be completing
  • will have completed
  • will complete
  • am completing
✅ will have completed - Correct! Future perfect for completion before a future time.

Deck 17

Advanced Mixed Conditionals

Mix any if-clause with any main clause
Past/present/future conditions with any result

Examples - Past cond -> present result

Past cond -> present result

If I had accepted that job, I would be living in Tokyo now.

Examples - Present cond -> past result

Present cond -> past result

If he were more confident, he would have asked her out.

Examples - Past perf -> present perf

Past perf -> present perf

If she had left earlier, she wouldn't be stuck in traffic.

Common Mistakes

❌ "If I would have seen him, I would say hello."

✅ "If I had seen him, I would have said hello."

💡 Past perfect in if-clause, not 'would have'.

❌ "If he was taller, he would have joined the team."

✅ "If he were taller, he would have joined the team."

💡 Use 'were' for all subjects in unreal present.

❌ "If I had more money, I would buy a house is 2nd conditional."

✅ "If I had more money, I would buy a house. (This is 2nd conditional, not mixed)"

💡 Mixed = past if-clause + present main, OR present if-clause + past main.

Exercise

If she _____ (take) that job, she _____ (live) in Tokyo now.

  • took / would live
  • had taken / would be living
  • had taken / would have lived
  • took / will live
✅ had taken / would be living - Correct! Past condition -> present result (mixed).

Get someone to

Deck 19

Advanced Passive (Reporting Verbs)

It + be + pp + that... / Subject + be + pp + to + verb
Formal passive structures for reporting

Examples - It is said...

It is said...

It is said that the company is closing.

Examples - He is believed to...

He is believed to...

He is believed to have left the country.

Examples - It was reported...

It was reported...

It was reported that the ship had sunk.

Common Mistakes

❌ "He is said to left yesterday."

✅ "He is said to have left yesterday."

💡 Perfect infinitive: to have + pp for past.

❌ "She is believed being innocent."

✅ "She is believed to be innocent."

💡 Passive + to + base verb, not -ing.

❌ "It was reported that the man died."

✅ "The man was reported to have died."

💡 Both structures are correct, but the second is more formal.

Exercise

The company _____ (rumour) _____ (go) bankrupt.

  • is rumoured / to go
  • is rumoured / to have gone
  • was rumoured / to go
  • has been rumoured / going
✅ is rumoured / to have gone - Correct! Passive + perfect infinitive for past event.

Deck 20

Inversion (Advanced Structures)

Not only...but also / No sooner...than / Hardly...when
Inversion after negative/time adverbials

Examples - Not only

Not only

Not only did she win, but she also set a record.

Examples - No sooner

No sooner

No sooner had I sat down than the phone rang.

Examples - Hardly

Hardly

Hardly had we left when it started raining.

Common Mistakes

❌ "Not only she won, but she also broke the record."

✅ "Not only did she win, but she also broke the record."

💡 Inversion after 'not only': auxiliary before subject.

❌ "No sooner I arrived than it started."

✅ "No sooner had I arrived than it started."

💡 No sooner + had + subject + pp.

❌ "Hardly I had left when the phone rang."

✅ "Hardly had I left when the phone rang."

💡 Hardly + had + subject + pp.

Exercise

No sooner _____ (we / arrive) _____ the power went out.

  • we arrived / than
  • had we arrived / than
  • we had arrived / when
  • did we arrive / than
✅ had we arrived / than - Correct! No sooner + had + subject + pp + than.

Deck 23

Advanced Tense Usage

Present perfect vs past simple / Future perfect continuous
Subtle tense choices for precision

Examples - Present perfect vs past

Present perfect vs past

I have visited Paris (experience) vs I visited Paris last year (specific time).

Examples - Future perfect continuous

Future perfect continuous

By June, I will have been studying for 3 years.

Examples - Past perfect vs past simple

Past perfect vs past simple

She had already eaten when he arrived.

Common Mistakes

❌ "I have seen him yesterday."

✅ "I saw him yesterday."

💡 Don't use present perfect with specific past time.

❌ "I have been knowing her for years."

✅ "I have known her for years."

💡 Stative verbs don't use continuous.

❌ "By next week, I will be working here for a year."

✅ "By next week, I will have been working here for a year."

💡 Future perfect continuous for duration up to a future point.

Exercise

By December, I _____ (work) here for five years.

  • will be working
  • will have been working
  • have been working
  • will work
✅ will have been working - Correct! Future perfect continuous for duration up to a future point.
