Introductions & Networking

Scenario

Networking Event

You're at a business networking event in Vienna. People from various companies are present. Introduce yourself, make small talk, and exchange business cards.

Key Phrases

Nice to meet you. I'm [Name], and I work as a [role] at [company].

Use: When meeting someone for the first time in a professional setting.

What brings you to this event?

Use: To show interest in the other person and start a conversation.

That sounds interesting. Could you tell me more about what your company does?

Use: To keep the conversation going and show genuine curiosity.

I'd love to stay in touch. Here's my business card.

Use: To end the conversation professionally and exchange contact details.

It was a pleasure meeting you. Let's connect on LinkedIn.

Use: Follow-up suggestion after the event.

Example Dialogue

Anna

Hi, I'm Anna. I work in marketing at TechVision. What about you?

Thomas

Nice to meet you, Anna. I'm Thomas, a developer at CodeCraft. What brings you to this event?

Anna

I'm looking to connect with people in the tech industry. We're launching a new product next month and I'm interested in potential partnerships.

Thomas

That sounds fascinating. Could you tell me more about the product?

Anna

Of course! It's a project management tool designed for remote teams. I'd love to get your perspective as a developer. Here's my card.

Thomas

Great, let's stay in touch. It was a pleasure meeting you.

Language Focus

Formal vs Informal Register

Formal: 'It's a pleasure to meet you.' / Informal: 'Hey, nice to meet you!'

Formal: 'What brings you to this event?' / Informal: 'So what brings you here?'

Formal: 'Could you tell me more about...' / Informal: 'Tell me more about...'

Start with formal language and become more relaxed as the conversation develops.

Meetings & Discussions

Scenario

Team Meeting

You're leading a weekly team meeting. Review progress, address blockers, and assign action items. Keep the meeting focused and productive.

Key Phrases

Let's start with a quick round of updates from everyone.

Use: Opening the meeting and setting the agenda.

Can you walk us through the progress on your task?

Use: Asking for a detailed status update.

What blockers are you currently facing?

Use: Identifying obstacles that need resolution.

How can we help move this forward?

Use: Offering support and resources.

Let's wrap up. Action items: [list]. Thanks everyone.

Use: Closing the meeting with clear next steps.

Example Dialogue

Manager

Let's start with updates. Sarah, how's the website redesign going?

Sarah

We're 80% complete. The main blocker is waiting for content from the client — it's a week overdue.

Manager

How can we help move this forward?

Sarah

If someone could follow up with the client directly, that would speed things up considerably.

Developer

I can send the email today and copy you on it.

Manager

Perfect. Action items: Sarah finishes the design by Thursday, Dev follows up with the client. Thanks everyone!

Language Focus

Managing Turn-Taking in Meetings

To interrupt politely: 'Sorry, can I just add something...'

To take the floor: 'If I could just finish my point...'

To hand over: 'I'd love to hear your thoughts on this.'

To bring focus: 'Let's get back on track.'

Effective meetings need clear facilitation and respectful turn-taking.

Presentations & Pitches

Scenario

Client Pitch

You're presenting a new project proposal to a potential client. Engage them with clear data, handle questions confidently, and close with a call to action.

Key Phrases

Good morning, thank you for your time today. I'm excited to present...

Use: Opening professionally with enthusiasm.

Today I'll walk you through our proposal for...

Use: Setting the agenda and outlining what you'll cover.

As you can see from the data on screen...

Use: Presenting evidence to support your points.

What makes us different is our approach to...

Use: Highlighting your unique value proposition.

I'd be happy to answer any questions you may have.

Use: Transitioning to Q&A confidently.

Example Dialogue

Presenter

Good morning, thank you for your time. Today I'll walk you through our digital marketing proposal for your e-commerce platform.

Presenter

'Moving on to the next point...' — transitioning between topics

'Let me give you an example of this...' — illustrating with specifics

'As I mentioned earlier...' — referring back to previous points

'To summarize what we've covered...' — recapping key takeaways

Signposting helps your audience follow your argument and stay engaged.

Phone Calls & Video Conferences

Client Call

You're calling a client to discuss a project update. The connection is poor and you need to be clear and professional despite technical difficulties.

Key Phrases

Can you hear me clearly? Let me just check the connection.

Use: Dealing with technical issues professionally.

I'll send you the documents after this call for your reference.

Use: Confirming follow-up actions.

Just to recap what we discussed to make sure we're aligned...

Use: Summarizing key points for clarity.

When would be a good time to schedule our next call?

Use: Moving to next steps efficiently.

Thank you for your time today. Speak soon.

Use: Professional closing.

Example Dialogue

You

Good morning, can you hear me clearly? The connection seems a bit unstable today.

Client

You

Language Focus

Telephone Phrases for Clarity

'Could you repeat that, please?' — asking for clarification

'I'll email you a summary of our discussion.' — confirming in writing

'Sorry, I didn't catch that — could you speak more slowly?' — dealing with poor audio

Clear communication prevents misunderstandings in phone/video calls.

Emails & Written Communication

Scenario

Professional Email

You need to write a formal email to a client explaining a project delay. Be transparent about the situation while maintaining confidence in your ability to deliver.

Key Phrases

I am writing to inform you about an adjustment to the project timeline.

Use: Stating the purpose of the email professionally.

Due to unforeseen circumstances beyond our control...

Use: Explaining the situation without making excuses.

We sincerely apologize for any inconvenience this may cause.

Use: Offering a formal apology.

To compensate, we would like to offer a 10% discount on your next order.

Use: Providing a concrete solution.

Please do not hesitate to contact me if you have any questions or concerns.

Use: Professional closing with availability.

Example Dialogue

Subject

Project Timeline Update — [Project Name]

Salutation

Dear Mr. Schmidt,

Body1

I am writing to inform you that the delivery date for your project has been moved to March 15th due to supply chain delays.

Body2

We sincerely apologize for any inconvenience this may cause. To compensate, we would like to offer a 10% discount on your next order.

Body3

Please do not hesitate to contact me if you have any questions.

Sign-off

Best regards, Project Manager

Language Focus

Formal Email Structure

Subject line: Clear and specific about the email's purpose

Salutation: Dear Mr./Ms. [Last Name] for formal emails

Opening: State your purpose directly

Body: Provide details, context, and solutions

Closing: Call to action and availability

Sign-off: Best regards / Sincerely / Kind regards

Negotiations & Deal-Making

Scenario

Price Negotiation

You're negotiating a contract renewal with a supplier. They've proposed a price increase. Push back diplomatically and reach a win-win agreement.

Key Phrases

We appreciate the quote, but we were hoping for a more competitive price.

Use: Opening negotiations diplomatically.

If we commit to a longer contract term, could you offer a volume discount?

Use: Trading concessions strategically.

We're in a position to move quickly if the terms are right.

Use: Creating urgency without pressure.

How about we meet in the middle at 12%?

Use: Finding compromise that works for both sides.

I think we have a deal. Let's finalize the details with legal.

Use: Closing the negotiation professionally.

Example Dialogue

Buyer

We appreciate the quote, but we were hoping for something more competitive. Your proposed increase is 18%.

Seller

Our costs have risen significantly. 18% is already quite competitive given market conditions.

Buyer

If we commit to a 2-year contract instead of 1, could you offer a volume discount?

Seller

We could do 10% for a 2-year commitment.

Buyer

How about 12% and we have a deal?

Seller

I think we can make that work. Let's finalize the details with our legal teams.

Language Focus

Negotiation Phrases for Win-Win Outcomes

'We were hoping for...' — softening your counter-position

'If we... could you...?' — trading concessions

'Let's find a middle ground.' — seeking compromise

'What if we...?' — proposing creative solutions

The best negotiations leave both parties feeling satisfied.

Complaint Handling

A customer is calling to complain about a delayed order. They're frustrated. Listen actively, show empathy, and offer a concrete solution.

Key Phrases

I completely understand your frustration, and I'm here to help.

Use: Showing empathy and taking ownership.

Let me look into this immediately and find the best solution for you.

Use: Taking quick action.

Use: Offering a concrete resolution.

I sincerely apologize for the inconvenience this has caused.

Use: Formal apology with accountability.

Is there anything else I can help you with today?

Use: Closing positively and ensuring satisfaction.

Agent

That's very kind. Thank you for resolving this.

Agent

Is there anything else I can help you with today?

Language Focus

De-escalation Techniques

Listen actively: 'I understand...' — validate their feelings

Acknowledge: 'That must be frustrating' — show empathy

Take ownership: 'Let me take care of this' — don't blame others

Offer options: 'Here's what we can do...' — give them control

De-escalation turns angry customers into loyal advocates.

Job Interviews

Scenario

Final Round Interview

You're in the final round for a marketing position at an international company. The interviewer is asking behavioral questions. Use the STAR method to structure your answers.

Key Phrases

My greatest strength is my ability to analyze data and translate it into actionable strategies.

Use: Highlighting a key qualification with specificity.

In my previous role, I managed a team of 8 across 3 time zones.

Use: Demonstrating leadership experience.

A challenge I overcame was launching a product with only 2 weeks' preparation time.

Use: Using STAR method: Situation + Task.

I'm passionate about this industry because I believe marketing can drive real business impact.

Use: Showing genuine enthusiasm and alignment.

Where I see myself in 5 years is leading a team and contributing to strategic decisions.

Use: Demonstrating ambition and long-term thinking.

Example Dialogue

Interviewer

Tell me about yourself and why you're interested in this role.

Candidate

I'm a marketing professional with 5 years of experience in digital campaigns. My greatest strength is my analytical approach to problem-solving. I'm passionate about this role because your company's values align with mine.

Interviewer

Can you give me an example of a challenge you overcame?

Candidate

In my last role, we had a major client presentation with only 2 days' notice. I organized the team, delegated tasks effectively, and we delivered an outstanding presentation that won the account.

Interviewer

Where do you see yourself in 5 years?

Candidate

I'd like to be leading a marketing team, mentoring junior staff, and contributing to strategic decisions at a senior level.

Language Focus

STAR Method for Behavioral Questions

Situation: Set the context briefly (1 sentence)

Task: What was your specific responsibility?

Action: What steps did you take? (the longest part)

Result: What was the positive outcome? Quantify if possible

Keep answers structured and concise — aim for 1-2 minutes.

Career Development

Scenario

Performance Review

Your annual performance review is happening. You need to discuss achievements, areas for growth, and future goals. Present your case confidently.

Key Phrases

I've been in my current role for 2 years and have consistently exceeded my targets by 15%.

Use: Establishing your track record with data.

I'd like to discuss my career progression and next steps.

Use: Opening the conversation proactively.

I've taken on additional responsibilities including mentoring two junior team members.

Use: Demonstrating growth and leadership.

What would I need to demonstrate to be considered for the senior role?

Use: Asking for clear criteria.

I appreciate your feedback and will work on those areas.

Use: Showing receptiveness to development areas.

Example Dialogue

Employee

Thank you for meeting with me. I've been in my current role for 2 years and have consistently exceeded my targets by 15%.

Manager

Yes, your performance has been excellent. What did you want to discuss?

Employee

I'd like to discuss my career progression. I've taken on additional responsibilities including mentoring junior staff and leading the Q3 campaign.

Manager

That's great to hear. To be considered for the senior role, you'd need to demonstrate strategic thinking and cross-team collaboration.

Employee

I appreciate your feedback. I'll work on those areas and would love to lead the upcoming cross-departmental project.

Language Focus

Talking About Achievements Without Bragging

Use data: 'increased by 15%' vs 'did well' — be specific

Use 'we' for team wins, 'I' for personal contributions

Acknowledge others: 'I was fortunate to work with a great team'

Be specific about your role: 'My contribution was...'

Confidence is good — just back it up with evidence.

Project Management

Scenario

Project Status Update

You're giving a project update to stakeholders. Be transparent about challenges while showing you have a plan to address them.

Key Phrases

The project is currently on track for delivery in Q3, with 75% of milestones completed.

Use: Giving a clear status update.

We've identified a potential risk with our supplier that could impact the timeline.

Use: Being transparent about challenges.

Our mitigation strategy involves qualifying a backup supplier within 2 weeks.

Use: Proposing concrete solutions.

We need approval to allocate additional budget for expedited shipping.

Use: Requesting resources clearly.

I take full responsibility for ensuring we meet the deadline.

Use: Showing accountability.

Example Dialogue

Stakeholder

How's the project going? Are we on track?

PM

The project is on track for Q3 delivery with 75% of milestones completed. However, we've identified a potential risk with our main supplier.

Stakeholder

What's the impact?

PM

If the supplier can't deliver on time, we could face a 3-week delay. Our mitigation strategy involves qualifying a backup supplier.

Stakeholder

What do you need from us?

PM

We need approval to allocate 15,000€ for the backup supplier. I take full responsibility for ensuring we meet the deadline.

Language Focus

Delivering Bad News Professionally

Be direct: 'Unfortunately, we're facing a challenge...'

Don't blame: Focus on the issue, not people

Always pair with solutions: 'Here's our mitigation plan...'

Show accountability: 'I'm taking responsibility for this'

Bad news delivered well builds trust and credibility.

Use: Opening the discussion openly.

Use: Identifying top priorities.

Can we delegate anything to free up capacity for critical work?

Use: Exploring delegation options.

Clear priorities prevent overwhelm and missed deadlines.

Problem Solving

Scenario

Crisis Management

A major issue has just emerged — your company's website is down during a peak sales period. Lead the team through a structured problem-solving process.

Key Phrases

Let's gather the facts first before we react.

Use: Staying calm under pressure.

What are our options here? Let's brainstorm.

Use: Encouraging collaborative problem-solving.

I think we should go with option B because it's the fastest to implement.

Use: Making a decision with reasoning.

Let's assign clear responsibilities for the next 24 hours.

Use: Delegating action items.

I'll escalate this to senior management if we can't resolve it within 4 hours.

Use: Knowing when to escalate.

Example Dialogue

Manager

We have a situation — the website is down and we're losing sales every minute. Let's gather the facts first.

Developer

It looks like a server overload due to the promotion traffic. We need to scale up immediately.

Manager

What are our options?

Developer

Option A: Scale up servers now — costs 5,000€ but resolves in 30 minutes. Option B: Put up a maintenance page while we optimize — free but loses sales for 2+ hours.

Manager

I think we should go with option A — we can't afford to lose peak traffic. Let's assign responsibilities.

Developer

I'll handle the scaling. Can someone communicate with affected customers?

Manager

I'll draft a customer communication and escalate to the CEO. Let's reconvene in 2 hours.

Language Focus

Problem-Solving Framework

1. Define the problem clearly — what exactly is wrong?

2. Gather information and data — don't guess

3. Brainstorm possible solutions — quantity over quality initially

4. Evaluate options and choose the best one

5. Implement, monitor, and adjust as needed

Structured problem-solving prevents panic and poor decisions.

Leadership

Scenario

Team Motivation

Your team is demotivated after a major project failed to meet expectations. Address the situation, acknowledge feelings, and rebuild confidence.

Key Phrases

I know the last project didn't go as planned, but I'm proud of the effort everyone put in.

Use: Acknowledging feelings and validating effort.

Use: Reframing positively.

Use: Involving the team in solutions.

I believe in this team's ability to succeed — we've overcome challenges before.

Use: Expressing confidence.

Let's set some clear, achievable goals for next quarter and support each other.

Use: Moving forward with actionable next steps.

Example Dialogue

Leader

Leader

Team Member

Thank you. We're ready to move forward.

Language Focus

Leadership Language for Motivation

Acknowledge: 'I understand how you feel...' — validate emotions

Reframe: 'This is an opportunity to...' — focus on growth

Involve: 'What do you think we should do?' — empower the team

Inspire: 'I believe in this team's ability to...' — express confidence

Motivational leaders focus on solutions, not blame.
