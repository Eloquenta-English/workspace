PART 1 — Interview

Part 1: Interview

The examiner asks questions about everyday topics. Candidates answer individually.

Format

Duration: 2 minutes (pair) / 3 minutes (group of 3)

Focus: Giving personal information, expressing opinions on familiar topics

Topics covered in this deck:

  • Sports and exercise
  • Music, films, and entertainment
  • Reading and hobbies
  • Gaming and online activities
  • Weekend activities and free time

Candidates should give full answers — not just "yes" or "no". Aim for 2-3 sentences per answer.

PART 1 — Practice
Timer: 2 minutes — Answer all questions

Interview Questions

Answer each question in turn. Speak in full sentences. The timer starts automatically.

  1. What do you do in your free time?
  2. Do you prefer spending your free time alone or with friends? Why?
  3. Have your hobbies changed since you were younger?
  4. Is it important for people to have hobbies? Why?
  5. What hobby would you like to try in the future?
Examiner tip

Don't memorise answers. The examiner can tell. Speak naturally and show your personality.

PART 1 — Model Answers

Sample Answers

Click each button to reveal a model answer. Try to say your answer first!

Model Answer: In my free time, I usually play football with my friends after school. I also enjoy watching videos online and listening to music. Sometimes I read books, especially adventure stories, because they help me relax after a busy day at school.
Model Answer: I definitely prefer spending time with friends because it's more fun. However, sometimes I need to be alone to study or just relax. I think it's important to have a balance between social time and personal time.
Model Answer: Yes, definitely. When I was younger, I loved drawing and playing with toys. Now I'm more interested in sports and technology. I think it's natural for hobbies to change as you grow up and discover new things.
Model Answer: Yes, I believe hobbies are very important because they help you relax and learn new skills. Without hobbies, life would be boring — you'd only work and study. Hobbies also help you meet people with similar interests.
Model Answer: I'd love to learn how to play the guitar. My cousin plays, and he always sounds amazing. I think it would be cool to be able to play music at parties. Maybe I'll start lessons next year if I can save enough money.
PART 2 — Long Turn

Part 2: Long Turn

Each candidate speaks for 1 minute about two photographs. Then the other candidate answers a short question.

Candidate A

Looks at two photos for 1 minute

Compares them and answers a question

Speaks for 1 minute

Candidate B

Listens to Candidate A

Answers a short question about the photos

Speaks for 30 seconds

Key skill

Compare the photos — don't just describe them. Use language of similarity and difference: "Both photos show...", "In the first photo... whereas in the second..."

PART 2 — Practice A
Timer: 1 minute — Compare the photos

Long Turn: Set A

Candidate A: Look at these two photographs. They show people doing different hobbies. Compare the photographs and say which hobby you think is more enjoyable.

Photo A: Playing football with friends outdoors

Photo B: Playing video games indoors on a computer

Follow-up (Candidate B)

Which hobby do you prefer? (30 seconds)

PART 2 — Model Answer A

Long Turn Model Answer

Model Answer: Both photos show people enjoying their free time, but in very different ways. In the first photo, there are some teenagers playing football outside on a field. They look very active and energetic. In the second photo, a young person is sitting at a desk playing video games on a computer. They seem very focused on the screen. I think the first hobby is more enjoyable because it involves being outdoors and socialising with friends. However, video games can be relaxing and help you develop problem-solving skills. Personally, I prefer activities that get me outside because I think it's important for your health to exercise in the fresh air.
Follow-up Answer: I definitely prefer playing video games because it's something I can do when the weather is bad, and I can play with my friends online even if they aren't at my house. Also, I think gaming helps me relax after studying.
PART 2 — Practice B
Timer: 1 minute — Compare the photos

Long Turn: Set B

Candidate B: Look at these two photographs. They show people enjoying music in different ways. Compare the photographs and say how you think the people are feeling.

Photo A: A teenager playing guitar in a band with friends

Photo B: A teenager listening to music with headphones while walking

Follow-up (Candidate A)

Do you prefer listening to music alone or with others? (30 seconds)

PART 2 — Model Answer B

Long Turn Model Answer

Model Answer: Both photos show teenagers enjoying music, but in different situations. In the first photo, a boy is playing guitar with his band. He looks very happy and confident because he's performing with his friends. In the second photo, a girl is listening to music through headphones while walking alone. She looks relaxed and peaceful. I think the people in the first photo are feeling excited because they're playing music together, while the person in the second photo is feeling calm and relaxed. I believe both ways of enjoying music are great, but I would prefer playing music with friends because it's more fun to share the experience.
Follow-up Answer: I prefer listening to music with others, especially at concerts or parties, because the atmosphere is amazing. However, sometimes I enjoy putting on my headphones and just relaxing by myself when I need to chill out after school.
PART 3 — Collaborative Task

Part 3: Collaborative Task

Candidates discuss a situation together and make a decision. The examiner gives a question with five prompts.

Format

Phase 1: 2 minutes — Discuss all five prompts together

Phase 2: 1 minute — Make a final decision together

Skills tested:

  • Exchanging ideas and opinions
  • Agreeing and disagreeing politely
  • Suggesting and speculating
  • Negotiating and reaching a decision
  • Justifying your choices

This is a discussion — talk TO each other, not to the examiner. Build on what your partner says.

PART 3 — Practice A
Timer: 2 minutes — Discuss all options

Collaborative Task A

Your school wants to organise a new after-school club for students. Here are some ideas and a question for you to discuss. First, talk to each other about how beneficial each activity would be for students. Then decide which activity the school should choose.

Discuss these options
  • ⚽ A football or basketball club
  • 🎨 An art and drawing workshop
  • 🎮 A gaming and esports club
  • 🎵 A music band or choir
  • 📚 A book club with discussions

Phase 2 (after timer): Decide together which activity would be most popular.

PART 3 — Decision
Timer: 1 minute — Make your final decision

Make Your Decision

Now decide together: which one activity should the school choose? Be ready to explain your reasons.

Useful language

"I think we should choose... because..."

"That's a good point, but I feel that..."

"How about we go with...?"

"I agree with you. Let's decide on..."

PART 3 — Model Answer A

Collaborative Task Model Answer

Model Discussion:

A: I think the football club would be great because it helps students stay fit and learn teamwork. What do you think?

B: That's a good point, but not everyone enjoys sports. I feel that an art workshop would be more inclusive because creative people could express themselves. Also, it's a good way to relax.

A: I see what you mean, however, a gaming club could be very popular with students who are into technology. It might help them develop skills like problem-solving too.

B: I agree that gaming is popular, but some parents might not like it. How about we go with the music band? It's creative AND social.

A: I agree with you. Let's decide on the music band — it gives everyone a chance to participate.
Decision: We decided that the school should choose the music band because it's a creative activity that allows students to work together and express themselves. Also, the school could organise concerts for parents, which would be a great community event.
PART 4 — Discussion

Part 4: Extended Discussion

The examiner asks further questions related to the Part 3 topic. Candidates discuss together and with the examiner.

Format

Duration: 4-5 minutes

Focus: Expressing and justifying opinions, agreeing/disagreeing, speculating about broader themes

Skills tested:

  • Discussing abstract and social issues
  • Expressing opinions with reasons and examples
  • Responding to and challenging partner's ideas
  • Speculating about the future
  • Using a range of vocabulary and structures

This is the highest-level part. Show you can discuss ideas, not just facts. Use phrases like "I think the main issue is...", "In my country...", "I suppose it depends on..."

PART 4 — Practice
Timer: 3 minutes — Discuss all questions

Extended Discussion

Discuss these questions together. Give your opinions and reasons. The timer starts automatically.

  1. Do you think young people spend too much time on screens these days? Why?
  2. Is it better to have hobbies that are active (like sports) or creative (like art or music)?
  3. How have hobbies changed for young people compared to their parents' generation?
  4. Do you think schools should make hobbies and free-time activities part of the school day?
  5. What do you think hobbies will look like in the future?
Top tip

Extend your answers with examples: "For example, in my school...", "I read that...", "My friend told me that..."

PART 4 — Model Answers

Discussion Model Answers

Model Answer: Yes, I definitely think many young people spend too much time on screens. For example, my friends often spend three or four hours a day on their phones or playing video games. I think the main issue is that screens are very addictive — apps and games are designed to keep you looking at them. However, it's not all bad because screens can also be educational if you use them in the right way.
Model Answer: I don't think one is necessarily better than the other — it depends on the person. Active hobbies like sports are great for your health and help you learn teamwork. Creative hobbies like art or music help you express yourself and reduce stress. Personally, I think it's good to have a mix of both so you can stay healthy and develop your creativity.
Model Answer: I think hobbies have changed a lot compared to our parents' generation. In the past, people spent more time outdoors and had hobbies like cycling or playing in parks. Now, many young people prefer indoor activities like gaming or watching videos. However, I think this is starting to change because more people are becoming aware of the importance of exercise and being outdoors.
Model Answer: Yes, I believe schools should offer more free-time activities during the school day. For example, my school has a one-hour sports period once a week, and it's something I really look forward to. It gives students a break from studying and helps them discover new talents. I think every school should provide at least one afternoon per week for clubs and hobbies.
Model Answer: I think in the future, hobbies will become more technology-based. For example, virtual reality gaming will probably become very popular, and people might go on virtual holidays instead of travelling. However, I also think there will be a movement towards traditional hobbies as people try to escape from technology. I suppose it depends on the person and what makes them happy.

Assessment Criteria

Candidates are marked on four areas, each scored 0-5:

Grammar & Vocabulary

Range and accuracy of grammar. Variety and appropriacy of vocabulary. Can you use complex structures?

Discourse Management

Can you organise your ideas clearly? Use linking words? Keep going without long pauses?

Pronunciation

Clear and understandable? Correct stress and intonation? Individual sounds accurate?

Interactive Communication

Do you respond to your partner? Start and maintain conversation? Ask for opinions?

  • I think... because...

Speculating

Buying Time

  • That's an interesting question...
  • Let me think about that...
  • Well, I'm not sure, but...
  • I haven't thought about that before, but...
  • Off the top of my head...
